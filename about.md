## What is Recall?

Recall Contacts is a free iPhone app that layers on top of your existing Apple Contacts. It lets you tag people with hashtags ŌĆö by place, by context, by moment, by relationship ŌĆö and find them instantly by searching those tags later.

It does not replace your phone's contacts app. It makes it dramatically more useful.

## The Problem

Most contacts apps are built around names. They assume you can spell the name of the person you're looking for. They sort alphabetically. They offer a search bar that requires you to know what you're searching for.

This works fine if you're organised, have a good memory for names, and meet people occasionally. It breaks down when:

- You meet a lot of people ŌĆö at events, on shoots, at school pickup
- You're dyslexic and can't always spell names reliably
- You have ADHD and names disappear from working memory fast
- You know someone by context ("the architect from the Tokyo dinner") rather than by name

Most people, if they're honest, remember people by context first. They remember the face, the story, the city, the event. The name comes second ŌĆö if at all.

Recall was built for this reality.

---

## The Solution

Tag your contacts with hashtags that describe how you know them:

- `#NYC #gallery #2024` for someone you met at an opening in New York
- `#schoolmum #Luca` for the mother of your son's classmate
- `#doctor #Harley` for your GP
- `#investor #SXSW` for someone you met at a conference

Later, you search any of those hashtags. Recall filters your contacts instantly. You find the person without needing to spell their name, without scrolling A to Z, without remembering which folder you put them in.

---

## The Philosophy

Recall is built on a simple belief: **memory works by association, not by alphabet**.

When you try to remember someone, you almost never recall their name first. You recall the context ŌĆö where you were, what they did, what you were talking about. The name comes later, if at all.

Traditional contacts apps ignore this completely. They demand the name. They sort by the name. They search the name.

Recall goes the other way. It takes context as the input and returns the name as the output. This is how memory actually works.

The app is also built on a commitment to privacy. Your contacts are private. They stay on your device. No account is required. No data leaves your phone.
